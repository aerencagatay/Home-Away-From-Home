Guidelines & Terms

Home Away From Home · Last updated April 2026

About the Exhibition

Milano: Home Away From Home is a contemporary art exhibition exploring migration, belonging, and the spaces between. Organised by a team of graduate students in Arts Management at SDA Bocconi, the exhibition will take place from 4 to 7 June 2026 at the Rifugio Antiaereo di Piazza Grandi, Milan.

This open call invites early-stage artists to submit existing or new work across all mediums for consideration by the curatorial team and advisory board.

Eligibility

  • Open to artists who have lived, studied, or worked in Milan at any point in their lives. You do not need to be currently based in Milan.
  • Applicants must be at least 18 years of age at the time of submission.
  • All artistic mediums and disciplines are welcome, including but not limited to painting, sculpture, photography, video, installation, performance, digital art, textile, ceramics, printmaking, sound art, and mixed media.
  • Both emerging and early-career artists are encouraged to apply. There is no requirement for prior exhibition history.

What to Submit

Each applicant may submit up to 3 works. For each work, please provide:

  • Title, year, classification, and medium
  • Up to 4 images (JPG, PNG, or TIFF · max 10 MB each · minimum 1500px on the longest side)
  • Optional: one video file per work (MP4 or MOV · max 100 MB)
  • Dimensions (H × W × D in cm) and weight (kg), if applicable
  • A written description of the work, including concept, materials, process, and — if relevant — its connection to Milan or themes of migration and belonging
  • Installation requirements or preferences

In addition, please upload:

  • A portfolio (PDF · max 20 MB) — required
  • A CV (PDF or DOC · max 5 MB) — optional
  • A website or portfolio URL — optional

Selection Process

All applications will be reviewed by the Home Away From Home curatorial team. Shortlisted submissions will be shared with the advisory board, who will provide expert guidance and serve as the jury for the exhibition prize.

Selection criteria include artistic quality, conceptual strength, relevance to the exhibition theme, and feasibility of installation within the venue.

All applicants will be notified of the outcome by email after the review process is complete.

Prize

One selected artist will receive a €500 award. Additional works may receive special recognition from the advisory board. The prize will be announced during the exhibition opening.

Key Dates

  • Application deadline: 26 April 2026, 23:59 CET
  • Notification of results: May 2026
  • Exhibition dates: 4–7 June 2026

Late submissions will not be accepted.
